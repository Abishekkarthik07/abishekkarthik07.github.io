# Research Assistant

Working as a Research Assistant under Dr. V. Pandiyaraju was one of the most formative parts of my undergraduate journey. What stood out to me was not just the technical depth of the work, but the way research demanded patience, discipline, and honesty with results. I learned that progress often came from long cycles of trial, error, and refinement rather than immediate breakthroughs. Handling noisy medical data, re-running experiments that failed to generalize, and questioning my own assumptions taught me to slow down and think critically about why a model behaved the way it did, not just how well it performed.
